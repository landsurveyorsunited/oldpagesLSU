Land Surveyors United Admin Checklist

The primary goals involved in being a network admin are to A) Get existing members to return to the network and share something or participate in discussions already on the network and B) Help members find where it is that they should be contributing to the network.  As we are working hard on getting funding and sponsorship for the network, it is important that we continue to assist in helping existing members become active contributors to the network and community.  With that said, I have written down a list of daily tasks that I myself try to do each and every day.  As an administrator, I am asking that you pick at least 5 tasks on this list and accomplish them each and everyday. Shortly down the road we hope to be able to pay moderators and admins for their commitment to the network.  Performing at least 5 simple tasks everyday will help that day come much sooner.


Reach Out and Introduce Yourself to at least 5 Members
You can run a search on the network in the top right for a place, topic, subject or person and find at least 5 other members that you would like to engage with.  Help them find their local group on the network and encourage them to start a new discussion in the forum and share something related to surveying in that place specifically.

Start a New Discussion Inside a Group Your Belong to or Join New Groups
Every single group on the network has its own forum inside.  You can find this forum by adding '/forum' to the end of the URL (for example: http://landsurveyorsunited.com/group/surveyorscanada/forum for Canada Land Surveyors Group). Every member has a unique set of groups that they belong to or should belong to based on Location, Type of Equipment and Types of Surveying they are involved with.

Add A Page to a Group
Only Moderators and Admins can add pages inside groups.  This means that pages can be created inside groups that can serve as a resource, a map, a set of videos, an embedded PDF or document or especially useful links to other resources.  For example, inside the United States Land Surveyors Group a page has been added for Latitude and Longitude of US...All 50 state groups in one place (a more advanced page)...and a facebook widget for US Land Surveyors.  You can actually go to any state board website and find useful links dedicated to land surveying in that state and create a page for them.  For groups that are not dedicated to a location, you can make pages dedicated to similar videos or photos.  For example, in the Topcon group, you could create a page that is dedicated to all photos on the network that have a topcon instrument featured in them.  Embed them into a page and call it something like "Land Surveyors United Members Using Topcon."  We have over 250+ different group forums.  Creating resource pages with a map is always a good idea! Here is a wizard for creating a map.  It is always a good idea to write a small descriptive paragraph of what the page will be about before embedding anything into the page so that search engines know what the page is about.

Upload Photos to the Network and give them proper titles, description, subject tags and Geolocation
Photos without descriptions, Titles and Tags do not help the network one bit.  This is why as moderators we must at times come behind a member who has not given their photos proper descriptions and change them for them (for example when someone uploads a photo through their phone.)  Simply find 5-10 photos which have bad titles and descriptions and click "Own this" dropdown and "Edit"  this will take you to the edit screen where you can add a proper title (ie. "Oklahoma Land Surveying" when the surveyor's profile shows they are in Oklahoma) a description of what is going on in the photo or type of equipment being shown in photo... change the URL to match the title with dashes (-) separating words (ie  oklahoma-land-surveying) and tags.  Tags are especially important because they serve as both a way to have photos with same tags grouped together in categories AND groups have a RSS feed inside them to automatically import photos and videos with a certain tag (ie photos tagged with OK automatically get sent to the Oklahoma group and show what surveying in Oklahoma is like visually)

Run a search on Youtube or Vimeo for a surveying related video-find a good one with a lot of views
and embed it into the network
You can find videos to share here or by searching for keywords on Youtube and Vimeo.  Sharing videos for surveying equipment types, mapping, surveying and geomatics generates the most engagement.  To embed a video, simply go to the video on Youtube and click Share>Embed grab the code and Paste it here.  Then go back to the video and click About, copy the description and replace the existing description in the edit page on the network (because it only imports the first 150 characters of Description. Last, add subject tags for that new imported video so that it can be grouped with others like it.

Find a member who has not contributed to the network in a while and reach out to them and invite them
to participate in a specific discussion
There are over 900,000 pages on LSU.  Everyday close to 20 new members join on the average.  That is close to 20 new people everyday who has never seen any of our discussions, photos, videos, or stories.  The more you get to know other members, the more you will know what types of topics they might be interested in.  Simply find an old discussion which still has relevance and add something to it.  You can also click "Own This" and Stop Featuring it, then refeature it and bring it to the top of the featured discussions.

Tell Us a Story.
Stories are essentially blog posts but because most surveyors do not have the slightest idea about what a blog is supposed to be, we call them stories.  Stories allow us to bring many different types of content into a single post.  For example, let's consider making a story about Survey Markers around the world.  Run a search for the tag Markers or Benchmarks in photos (you can do the same in videos and forums).  Now start a new story and take the embed codes from the photos and videos and copy paste them into the HTML version of the post editor- write a story around the photos and videos and post it.  You can then share it with the members who's photos and videos you used in the story (as they will tend to share these discussions with their friends and family on other networks).  The truth is you can use this method to write stories about ANYTHING without having to even shoot a video or photo.  Mine Your Experience! Feature or Re-Feature Content
When a particular piece of content is "featured" on the network, it is basically displayed as among the best or most useful in a particular feature.  For example, featured photos are those which are especially good or interesting, with good titles, tags and descriptions.  Before you feature or re-feature content you can edit it to make it more useful to the network by adding '/edit' to the end of the URL and correct any mispellings, add a photo of the subject if you have one or we have one on the network then republish and feature it to bring it to the top. With over 900,000 pages there is an endless amount of content to work with.  Only admins and moderators can edit content submitted by members...and members can edit only their own content.

Share Content on Facebook, Google+, Twitter, Linkedin and Elsewhere
If you haven't yet synced your profile with other social media accounts, you are missing out.  Land Surveyors United enables you to post simultaneous status updates to all of your social media channels with a single click.  When you have your profile synced you can also share anything on the network with a single click.  For example, you can click Tweet This and it will automatically generate a twitter message with title of post and link back to the content.  This is a terrific way to attract like minded surveyors to your profile, build a following and blast your reputation online into the stratosphere!
