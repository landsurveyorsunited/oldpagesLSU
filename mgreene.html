France May 2012 Issue


ARTS MAGAZINE
Commissioned Artist | Prints made from Flash 3d | Profile Article as it appears in Arts Magazine

Image making for me started with a photocopy machine making BMX Fanzines at the end of the 80s as a teenager in the suburbs of New York City. At the time my mentor was Andy Jenkins Creative Director/Editor of Freestylin Magazine in Los Angeles. Andy asked me to be involved with an Art Collective called "The Basement", which was short lived but made up of people like Todd Swank Mark Lewman and Spike Jonze to name a few. We did an Exhibition in a small gallery in Central California, the year was 1990. To make my work more valid I needed to study art. At the Time I was living in Cleveland Ohio



I pretended I was a student at the Cleveland institute of Art and read everything I could in the Library From Faith Ringold to Foucault and painted I then moved to the Bay Area and would help hang paintings at Asake Bomani Gallery S.F. at this point I was painting work inspired by Dubbufet, Basquiat and Schnabel the year was 91-92. By the winter Of 93 I moved back to New York. The Paintings I was making had matured significantly, but by 95 96 I became obsessed with the potential for experimentation in Hip hop creating music from an art world I was inspired by. In 97 I became part of an Experimental rap Group named Antipop Consortium under the Alias M. Sayyid. In 2006 I started learning Flash and making 2d animations.
The Digital Work matured

Symbolism / Digital Flyer

Top: Awareness in the Fourth Dimension is a title I thought would be good to describe some of the new Antipop Consortium material and my colleuges agreed. we needed a visual symbol that would be an effective way to communicate the idea of 4 dimensions across social media platforms.. "show not tell" came to mind, I sketched out some ideas in Flash and this is one of the things that seemed to fit the best. The triangles subtily represent an A for Awareness and an A for Antipop, exploding out are the colors of the universal spectrum. Bottom: A still from the Flash 2d Animation Special Forces.
