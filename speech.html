Speech Recognition in the Browser

With Dictation, you can use the magic of speech recognition to write emails and documents in the browser without touching the keyboard.

The app internally uses the built-in speech recognition engine of Google Chrome to transform your voice into digital text. It works offline as well.

Speak in your Native Language

You don't have to speak in English as Chrome's engine can recognize quite a few languages including Arabic, Chines, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Malay, Indonesian and more. Dication will automatically determine your browser's default launguage and uses it for subsequent transcriptions. Indian languages aren't supported at this time.

The x-webkit-speech attribute of HTML5 is only implemented for <input> (single-line) form fields so Dictation uses a workaround. It hides the speech-enabled input behind the microphone icon and appends the transcribed text to a regular DIV.

The tool was created by Amit Agarwal on August 10, 2012.

Online Dictation

Press Ctrl+Shift+.(period) or click the microphone icon and start talking. If the transcribed text is incorrect, just click the word to make a correction.

Dictation Tips

You can narrate long sentences in one go and Chrome's built-in speech recognition will transcribe them right - see video demo. Say "new line" to start a new paragraph or "delete everything" to completely erase your writing canvas.

HTML5 Speech Recognition

Dictation uses the x-webkit-speech attribute of HTML5 that is only implemented in Google Chrome. You can click the microhone to enter dictation mode or just use the keyboard shortcut Ctrl+Shift+.(period).

Practical Uses

Dictation converts your spoken voice into digital text. You can use it to write long forum posts, narrate stories or even compose a tall tweet. Just remember to copy the transcribed text before exiting the site.
