  1. Getting Started

    After signing up for Land Surveyors United, you might be overwhelmed at where to start or where to jump in. With over 4900+ members to date, it's not difficult to find yourself lost at first.  Make sure that your member profile is complete and reflects your accomplishments and specializations as a geospatial professional.  Pay special attention to your "location" question, as it will be your marker for the Global Member Map. You can also add yourself to the member map here.  Then, Ask yourself one of the following questions:

What do I specialize in and what can I share?
What kind of techniques or technologies should I learn?
From there, head over to either our +Curate or Explore page and find topics, meet other professionals and/or upload some of your own content to the network, such as photos and videos. Every member will also join a unique combination of group forums based on their location, types of equipment they use and the type of surveying they are familiar with. Run a quick search here and join the groups which apply to you. When starting discussions, try to remember to post in the group forum which best applies to your question in order to get the most replies.  If you are looking for inspiration on a topic to write about, check out our Land Surveying A to Z page and pick one, or suggest a term to be added.

  1. Introducing Yourself

    When you join any professional network, one of the first things you should always do is find the place where everyone is making introductions.  That place is here. Simply add a new post and Introduce yourself and tell the network where you are from, what you specialize in, what you care to learn from the network and share any special projects or ideas that you have.  You can include photos and videos that you uploaded if you like.  You'll meet a few like-minded associates just doing this step.  You can also learn what each member badge means here.

On Land Surveyors United, you will find that our content is structured in a way similar to a library.  Like the sections of a library where you would find types of content in groups, we have Group Forums based on Locations, Types of Surveying and Surveying Equipment Types.  Asking questions in the appropriate group forum will ensure that you get replies from members who are also familiar with the topic.  Join a group forum based on your location and network with other surveyors in your area.  Group forums are also accessible by our mobile interface, so staying connected has never been so simple.

  1. Contributing Content

    There are many levels of contribution on a network like Land Surveyors United.  The first level is simply commenting and sharing content already on the network.  When you see or read something which interests you, simply click favorite, send a twitter share or share it on your facebook page. We have made this super simple for you, as you can join your social media profiles with your account on LSU, by going here. You only need to do this once and with one click you can share anything on the network to other social media sites. In addition, you'll find it to be highly productive when posting status updates from the mobile app because you can post once and update all of your social profiles at the same time. The second level of participation is uploading photos and videos from your computer or from your phone ,while on the go.  Photos are the cornerstone for a great explanation of what your day-to-day surveying life is like.  Please add proper titles, tags, descriptions and locations to your photos, so that other members understand them. Third level of participation makes Land Surveyors United your own.  Its always a good idea to share a surveying story, add a new discussion, upload or embed a video or upload photos from the field.  Remember that our stories section of the site is different from the forum, as stories are longer and should be filled with examples and details.

  2. Build Your Reputation

    Every single time you contribute something new to our network, you are building your reputation online.  The type of reputation you build is entirely up to you.  On Land Surveyors United, we help surveyors build a solid positive reputation by giving you all of the tools and resources you will need to share helpful and useful materials for other surveyors to learn from until the end of time. Read some of our testimonials to see how other surveyors and geospatial professionals are using the network to stay productive and building long lasting relationships on and off the field.

  3. Welcome to Land Surveyors United!  Enjoy and Share!
