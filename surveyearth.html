Most of us know what a surveyor looks like.  Yet, have no idea about what a land surveyor is- much less, what a land surveyor does.

What is Survey Earth in a Day?

On the day of the solstice, June 21, 2013, geospatial professionals around the world and members of Land Surveyors United (a global support network for land surveyors) will be simultaneously recording survey grade GPS data from thousands of points around the globe, in order to gain a more accurate understanding of the earth's surface. This will be the second event of its kind; Survey Earth in a Day 2.0!

 

Brief Bio and Backstory

I am a graduate student of Applied Anthropology and web community developer who specializes in geospatial industry related communities. The land surveying industry was not built with a communication platform. So, I installed one. I enjoy accomplishing the impossible with no budget.  But this year, my members deserve more.  Giving them this opportunity to connect with other professionals like themselves is my responsibility.  

Did You Know...

Surveying is the second oldest profession known to man. My network Land Surveyors United is the world's first and only global social support community for land surveyors. As you might imagine, land surveyors have most likely the least access to the internet at work than most other industries. Therefore, a social network for land surveyors tends to grow slower than most other types of niche networks. We are currently pushing 5000 members, making it the world's largest social network for land surveyors. Everyday, surveyors around the world upload their photos from the field, videos, share stories and discuss surveying.

So..

I aim to help my members achieve one of the most difficult feats known to the profession-namely, creating public awareness regarding the importance of professional land surveyors to society. In order to do this, I need to reach as many professional land surveyors around the world as possible to participate in this event. The GPS data to be recorded is of great importance to not only the industry, but to science and to our understanding of the surface of the earth. Only professional land surveyors are trained to attain the GPS precision needed to tie all of this data together and map it to the real world

 

Last year on the solstice (Survey Earth in a Day), over 1700 surveyors participated in the event, however we operated with virtually no budget. Our bootstrap method of accumulating data via upload to our single server left us with many submissions which timed out and did not fully make it to us. This year, we have an entirely new plan, but we need your help.

The Impact of Survey Earth in a Day

As a community, we will be re-measuring the entire surface of the earth in a single day. This data will go towards surveying education worldwide and will hopefully change much of what we know about the surface of the earth, presently.

This project is very important not only to the professional land surveyors around the planet but also to you, the contributor and to the world.  Our results will contribute to a better understanding of Earth's surface. 

Participants are in place and ready for the event.  I have already done the hard work of building the world's first International Surveyors Network and Community, over the past 6 years. The consensus among all of my members is the need for public awareness and something monumental which they can all participate in order to promote this awareness.
